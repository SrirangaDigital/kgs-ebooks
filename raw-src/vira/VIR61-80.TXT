@H3E = 61. A Walking Encyclopaedia of<R>
Vedanta, Our ``Simple Humble'' Guruji

@ENG = When I entered the Vedanta Hall, the ``<B>Central Figure<D>'' 
was looking at me with affectionate looks, and asked me to sit near 
him. I was hesitating. He insisted. I sat. After hearing briefly about 
myself, he told me ``Why don't you come daily''. I said ``I will try 
whenever I am free''.

@ENG = From that day, now it is about <B>two years plus<D>, and I 
am <B>regularly attending<D> making myself free for his Upanishads 
and Brahma Sutra classes and getting the benefits. This knowledge 
is ``<B>the mother of all knowledges<D>'' as it is said, and it cannot 
be learnt except in this way through a Guru. Many in the class are 
having long years of continued exposure to his classes and they quote 
Gita or Upanishad mantras at appropriate instances.

@ENG = Our Guruji is very Simple, Humble, Knowledgeable, a walking 
Encyclopaedia of Advaita Vedanta. He is thorough in Sanskrit Grammar 
and also quotes Kalidasa and other epics. He can fluently lecture 
in Sanskrit, English and can speak Telugu also. Never forgets to remember 
his sixteen long years with his Guruji <B>Sri Satchidanandendra Saraswati 
Swamiji<D>, whose painting is adoring the hall next to <B>Sri Adi 
Shankaracharya<D>. He is punctual to class and never skips a day, 
whether sun, rain or cold. Encourages and helps everyone to recite 
and understand the difficult subject of Advaita Vedanta in simple 
Kannada.

@ENG = We pray for the long energetic life of <B>The Veera Vedanti<D> 
for spreading of Vedantic knowledge.
